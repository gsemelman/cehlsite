SEASON 1 PREVIEW
NORTHWEST DIVISION
COLORADO AVALANCHE- The Avs are a very good team.  Leading the way on forward are superstars like Forsberg, Sakic, Samsonov and Hull.  The Avs defense is lead by names such as Sydor and Mclaren, while a recent trade with Edmonton sent away Blake, the recieved Niinima in return.  The the net is guarded by Roberto Luongo who is a young talent that will be a force in the league for years to come.  Overall, Colorados defense is very well rounded, and their forward lines are rock solid with one of Hejduk, Lehtinen or Hull playing on the third line.
Colorado should win their division with ease.  But don't count the others out.
TOTAL POINTS PREDICTION: 110
EDMONTON OILERS- The Oil are a team that could be good, or could be a flop.  They recently acquired Rob Blake but lost Niiniima and Smyth which could prove to be costly.  Also the Oilers are rumoured to be shopping goaltender Tommy Salo around the league.  This could be the deal that makes or breaks this teams season, depending on the return.  Will they go young? Or will they make a playoff push?  If they do go young, this prediction will be wrong, however it is believed GM Matt Bull wants to go to the playoffs and not watch them on CBC.
TOTAL POINTS PREDICTION: 81
VANCOUVER CANUCKS- The Vancouver Canucks come in to this season with high hopes.  They have been called a one line team, but GM Ryan Williams has tried to get a solid second line in there.  The acquisition of Ray Whitney for the Sedins was a way to try and get scoring on the second line.  The Vancouver defense can be good, but also can be bad.  They are still somewhat young, but with the experience of Robert Svehla, maybe they can get the job done in key situations.  Dan Cloutier is also a question mark.  He has potential to be a star goalie, but will he ever get there is the question that the Canucks want answered. In this division the Canucks are stuck behind the Avalanche and need to have a miracle season to keep up with them.
TOTAL POINTS PREDICTION: 88
MINNESOTA WILD-  The Wild's situation is simple, they are building.  The Wild have a great future in players such as Gaborik, Bouchard, Koivu, and Kuba, but for now they are a team on the outside looking in.  Manny Fernendez will guard the fort while in front of him is a surprisingly good defense, for a building team anyways.  Overall, Minnesota is not a half bad team and could make a charge if they get on a roll sometime during the season.  However they will rely on young gun Marion Gaborik for most of the teams offense.
TOTAL POINTS PREDICTION: 70
CALGARY FLAMES- The Flames are currently a work in progress.  They recently traded Jarome Iginla to Tampa Bay in hopes for more depth.  They did get depth, but it is still not enough to make this team much better, just more rounded.  Now that Iginla is gone, Drury is the Calgary offense and if he doesn't put up numbers, they will be a horrible team.  Their defense is young and promising and will carry this team someday, but they are not ready yet.  Roman Turek will be facing many shots and will have to put up not only Vezina numbers, but Hart numbers for Calgary to stand any chance of making the playoffs.
TOTAL POINTS PREDICTION: 53
