CEHL PLAYOFF DRIVE

 

The CEHL regular season is quickly arriving to its end, while many teams are desperately clawing away for a chance to reach the post-season.  Some teams have had disapoointing seasons thus far, but will look to hit their stride just as we enter the highly anticipated stretch drive.  Other teams have surprised everyone this year, and will continue to try and prove themselves as a threat.

 

 

EASTERN CONFERENCE

 1. TORONTO MAPLE LEAFS 

  2. PHILADELPHIA FLYERS

 

The Flyers arrived in the post season for the first time in Season two, and faced the number 2 ranked Ottawa Senators in round 1.  The signing of Dominik Hasek had Flyers fans believing, but the series took an unexpected offensive turn and the Flyers were knocked out in a thrilling 7 game series.  Toronto is fighting for the Eastern Conference’s top spot again, which they held after last season.  After a disappointing first round exit in season 1, the Leafs took a nerve racking 6 game series from Pittsburgh in round one, and were confident playing against Atlanta is round 2.  However, the thrashers jumped out 3-0 in the series, but the Leafs comeback was far from enough, falling in 6 games.

 

These teams have never faced each other in the playoffs.

 

2.  BOSTON BRUINS 

7. NEW JERSEY DEVILS

 

The Bruins have had a very surprising season this year, following a 6th place finish last year and a quick first round exit at the hands of the Devils.  In season one, the Bruins finished 5th and advanced to the Conference finals before being stopped by the Devils there as well.  The Devils won the CEHL championship in season one, and finished 3rd last season.  They knocked Boston out in 5 games, but were dethroned by the Ottawa Senaors who took down the mighty Devils in 7 games.  This season, New Jersey has continued its downward spiral, and look to be a fringe team.  This playoff series would be one the Bruins crave, as they look to get revenge on the Devils, and possibly even give this once elite team its final goodbye.

 

Previous Playoff Meetings: Season One Eastern Finals, NJ 4, BOS 2, Season Eastern Conference Quarter-Finals NJ4, BOS 1

 

3. PITTSBURGH PENGUINS

6. BUFFALO SABRES

 

This would be the match-up of two of the rising teams in the CEHL. Pittsburgh made the playoffs last season, holding onto the final spot, but being pushed aside by the Leafs.  Pittsburgh, a team of concentrated strength in Scott Niedermayer, Mario Lemieux and Curtis Joseph leading this team alone, has rose all the way up to third position this season.  They are still within striking distance of the top spot, which would be an unbelievable achievement for this team.  The Sabres have been on the verge of exploding since season one, and it finally happened this season.  They only trail Pittsburgh by 2 points, so their current spot can change overnight.  The Sabres would love to get a high playoff spot and have home ice advantage for their first playoff appearance.  They are a dangerous team loaded with quick Europeans.

 

These teams have never met in the post-season.

 

4. MONTREAL CANADIENS

5. OTTAWA SENATORS

 

Both of these teams sit tied in points with Buffalo, so obviously these close standings can and will change very often.  However, this would make for a very interesting first round match-up.  The Senators struggled for a while at the beginning of the season, but a mammoth trade to land Milan Hejduk, Peter Forsberk and Keith Carney changed the face of last seasons Eastern Conference Finalists.  Days later they traded shaky goalie Patrick Lalime and surprisingly traded Sheldon Souray for proven goalie Evgeni Nabokov and defensive specialist Rob Zamuner.  Montreal have yet to make the playoffs, but stuck by their team this season, and it seems to be paying off.  Eric Lindros looked to be on his way out of town, but the team has been playing to well to break anything up.  Ottawa seems to be quickly on the rise, while Montreal is keeping steady, so the Senatord will likely finish higher in sich a close race when everything is done.

WESTERN CONFERENCE

 

1.VANCOUVER CANUCKS

8. COLUMBUS BLUE JACKETS

The Canucks have been the most dominate team in the CEHL this season, and they recently became the first team to clinch a post-seaosn birth.  Vancouver is comfortably in the playoffs, but are in a close race with Anaheim for first place.  The Canucks will finish with one of the top two spots, so the rest of this season still has meaning to keep them sharp.  The Blue Jackets were expected to have a better season this year, but the still struggling team has inched its way into the 8th spot.  There is still a long road ahead, but the franchise is creating a hockey fever in Columbus for the first time.  Even though these teams have never had a meaningful meeting, Vancouver GM Ryan Williams would love to knock out Columbus.  Both him and Columbus GM Matty Costante play in the same beer hockey league, where Costante’s team is far superior.  Williams would love to see his Canucks walk over the Jackets it round one, but Columbus have a scrappy team built for the playoffs.

 

These teams have never met in the playoffs.

 

2. ANAHEIM MIGHTY DUCKS

7. CHICAGO BLACKHAWKS

Anaheim look to continue their reign as the CEHL’s super power this season, after last season ended within a stones throw from being crowned champions.  Anaheim won the Western Conference Championship last season, before losing in 6 games to the Cinderella Atlanta Thrashers.  Anaheim is continuing to play as a super power should, and another playoff run should be expected.  Chicago seem to be in the playoffs, barring a late season melt down.  They sit 7 points ahead of 9th place Calgary, and only 3 away from 5th place Los Angelas.  The Blackhawks will enter their 3rd straight playoff as a mediocre team, and were knocked out by the big shots in the previous 2 seasons.

 

Previous Playoff Meetings: Season 2 Western Conference Semi-Finals, ANA 4-2

 

3. DETROIT RED WINGS

6. ST. LOUIS BLUES

An obvious perfect match for ESPN coverage, the St. Louis Blues and Detroit Red Wings would make for a tight and exciting series.  The Blues have history on their side, making the Western Final for the last two seasons, but losing both.  They are a depleted squad this time around, but still have the intimidating duo of Al MacInnis and Chris Pronger to contend with.  Detroit look to make the playoffs for the first time under GM Mike Clark.  The were the 8th placed team in season 1, after getting rid of their top players to get under the cap.  In season 2, Clark took over and completely changed the team.  After many interesting moves, and some key signings, the Red Wings have arrived on the playoff scene as a serious threat.  Joe Thornton and Saku Koivu frighten everyone they face, and the recent stockpile of experienced veterans puts Detroit in a good position for a playoff run.  They will look to play out the stretch and not lose the 3rd spot to San Jose, but likely won’t catch Anaheim or Vancouver.

 

These teams have never met in the playoffs.

 

4. SAN JOSE SHARKS

5. LOS ANGELAS KINGS

The Sharks have been a team unable to prove themselves come playoff time, and with a strong team this season, they would be devastated to have another first round exit.  In season one they got by Dallas in round one before being eliminated in round 2.  Last season, the 3rd ranked Sharks were shocked by the Blackhawks who got by them in 6 games.  Los Angelas have got to their first playoffs in franchise history, assuming they don’t have a major brain cramp the rest of the way.  Controversial GM Sean Sackrider has brushed many of the leagues GMs the wrong way, but a playoff victory would be a sweet victory for him.  Los Angelas definitely are a team that could win a series or possibly even two, so Sackrider could get last laugh on a few guys.  If they continue to play well, they could even close the 5 point gap between them and San Jose, so they haven’t counted out home ice advantage in Hollywood.

 

These teams have never played in the playoffs.
