CANADIAN ELITE HOCKEY LEAGUE

SEASON 7 PREVIEW

 

The sixth installment of the CEHL was one full of surprise, overachievement, underachievement’s forgotten combining for one of the most competitive seasons yet.

First, the overachieving Philadelphia Flyers took the league by storm, roaring out of the gate led by Johan Hedberg in net and Antoine Vermette attacking.  The second-half was no walk in the park; they had the league guessing as to whether or not the way they started was good enough to hang them in the playoffs.  The Flyers were able to hold onto 7th, and gave the Islanders a scare.  However, after leading the series 3-2, Philadelphia snapped out of their underdog dreams, getting discarded by a combined score of 12-4 in the concluding games.

In the West we saw the emergence of the patient Coyotes, who were once considered a joke of a franchise.  Led Vezina winner Dominik Hasek, the Coyotes rolled into their first playoff appearance in three seasons, locking down 6th.  A strong first round showing knocked out the favoured Calgary Flames in five (who are a success story in their own right), before being sent to the links by the Western Champion Detroit Red Wings in six games.

The Devils saw another notch on their belt buckle loosen, after an Atlantic Division title couldn’t get them past their rivals from Madison Square.  After winning the first game, they were downed the next four, including a 7-2 humiliating decisive blow.

Change came to one of the league’s most close-knit teams, as the Vancouver Canucks shipped out Joe Thornton in a deal to land Vincent Lecavalier, and Miikka Kiprusoff the game-breaking goalie they had dreamed of.

In the end, it came down to the Detroit Red Wings, Western Finalists in season 5 led by Jaromir Jagr, and the Ottawa Senators, who had been eliminated in round one as a 1st placed team in season’s 4 and 5, led by Joe Sakic.

A 7-1 win for Detroit in game one showed their might, but a 4-1 answer from Ottawa put the Red Wings back in their place.  After taking a 2-1 series lead in game 3 at home, the Senators shook the hopes of the Wings when Ryan Smyth scored at 10 minutes of the first overtime. 

A 4-2 win in the final game almost came as no surprise, as the Senators kept the Cosby cup in Ontario, with their first Championship.

 

But with season 7 on the rise many changes have occurred, and questions must be answered, which one of the upstart teams will be back, and who will be next?

Ottawa Senators

45-33-4-2

2nd in Northeast.  4th in East.

 

In Review:  The curse of the Senators was lifted in Season six, when they were crowned Cosby Cup champions after two seasons of trademark first round exits to inferior teams.  They came back from the brink in round one, beating Florida in the final two games of that series, including a 5-0 shutout to dust them off.  A second round defeat of the powerhouse Islanders, and an Eastern Conference Final championship over the Maple Leafs, both in six games, led them to the finals against the Red Wings.  After getting blown away 7-1 in the opening game, the Sens won the next four.  Ryan Smyth’s OT winner in game four was a back breaker leading into the 4-2 game five victory.  In their most unlikely season yet, the Sens finally grabbed their first banner.

 

Changes: The biggest change was also the ones leaving Sens fans without any hair.  Joe Sakic, the team’s leader and best player, left via free agency for Toronto.  No one in Ottawa saw this coming, but one thing is for sure, Joe has made a lot of enemies in the nation’s capital.  Saku Koivu was brought in to fill the void in a trade for the overpaid Milan Hejduk.  Matt Cooke, Rhett Warrener and Michal Handzus were also brought in through trades, while Kris Draper, Darryl Sydor and Roman Hamrlik were brought in through free agency.  The Sens showed an emphasis on defence this offseason, filling holes that should have them back near the top again.
